Best Chromebook for Kali Linux

The most appealing characteristic of Chromebooks is their low price. The price of a laptop is heavily influenced by the software licensing required to install Windows or Mac OS. On the other hand, Chromebook comes with Google's Chrome OS that is based on Linux and costs a fraction of the price. It includes Chrome OS which makes a Chromebook a Chromebook.

Chrome OS is optimized to run the suite of Google applications like Google Drive. Google Docs and Gmail. Unfortunately, non-Google software, like Microsoft Office or Photoshop, has limited or with compatibility on the Chromebook.

While several people take to Chromebooks like a fish to water, others may find their dependency on Google apps restrictive. As a result, a growing community of Chromebook users has begun to install Linux-based distros on their devices.

Many Chromebooks are ideal Linux candidates. Their components are usually powerful and capable, so we won't have to pay extra money on an operating system software license that won't be utilized anyhow.

In order to find the best Chromebook for Kali Linux, we will need to figure out which features are important to you and your home computing setup, as well as ensure that the laptop you purchase is capable of running Kali Linux.

We recommend Chromebook laptops with high-performance processors and enough RAM to allow you to run numerous applications simultaneously. Consider the form factor as a light and thin laptop is preferable for portability and transport. Always seeking for a strong processor, the ability to boot into the Linux operating system, and ability to boot into the Linux operating system, and a long-lasting battery when purchasing a Chromebook for Kali Linux.

Features You Need to Check Before Purchasing the Best Chromebook for Kali Linux

Performance is not the only factor to consider when purchasing a Chromebook for Kali Linux. You need to check the connectivity options, storage capacity, and durability to ensure that it is more versatile and long-lasting.

The following is a comprehensive list of features before purchasing the best Chromebook for Kali Linux.

  1. Storage capacity
  2. Design and durability
  3. Battery life
  4. Display technology
  5. Warranty
  6. Connectivity options

1. Storage Capacity

Chromebooks are not meant for graphics-intensive tasks like gaming or running large applications. As a result, as compared to traditional laptops, Chromebooks have limited storage space. They usually have 32GB or 64GB of storage space. On the other hand, Chromebooks give you quick access to Google Drive cloud storage. Some models additionally have a year's worth of free Google Cloud storage.

2. Design and Durability

Chromebook, in general, is designed to be portable devices with slim and attractive designs and weigh less than 3 pounds. Many variants weigh only 1 to 1.5 pounds for maximum portability and can be used as tablets. The fact that they are convertible adds to their versatility. In addition, the Chromebooks have appealing shapes and colors that make them appealing to the eye. Not only design, you should also consider the durability of the Chromebook and its parts. As they are portable devices, you can't help, but drop them once in a while. Chromebook feature military-grade construction which makes them sturdy to meet MIL-STD standards. These Chromebooks can endure a few drops and bumps without causing any major physical damage.

3. Battery Life

The majority of Chromebooks have a battery life of up to 10 hours. Some variants have a battery life of up to 14 hours. If we are not heavy users, anything over 6 hours should be enough. The battery life of Chromebooks will be mentioned for mixed usage.

When charging the battery, you should also pay attention to how long it takes to reach full capacity. You don't want to waste time charging the Chromebook when you could be utilizing it.

4. Display Technology

The visual quality of a Chromebook is determined by the display technology and the screen size. They have a display that is either HD or Full HD. The HD display can support up to 1280 x 720 pixels, while the Full HD display can display up to 1920 X 1080 pixels. If you want to use your Chromebook mostly for online streaming of movies and TV shows as well as video conferencing, a Full HD display is a way to choose. On the other hand, the HD display, will still deliver a decent viewing experience. The Chromebooks also come with the newest display technology, including IPS panel and anti-glare coating, which improve the viewing experience even more.

5. Warranty

No matter how durable they are, Chromebooks can still be damaged if they are dropped. Furthermore, they are vulnerable to electronic failures. As a result, it's crucial that the Chromebook comes with a guarantee. While some models have warranties of up to five years, we recommend getting a Chromebook with at least a one-year warranty.

6. Connectivity Options

There are only a few connectivity options available for Chromebooks. With a Chromebook, you won't find HDMI ports or DisplayPort. They have USG ports, as well as Bluetooth and Wi-Fi connectivity options. Some versions have a maximum of three USB ports to accommodate numerous connections. Also, check the standard/version of the connectivity options for quick and reliable connections.
