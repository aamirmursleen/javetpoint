Dropbox Alternatives

Users can store their images, movies, and documents online with the help of Dropbox. There are many reliable, secure substitutes to Dropbox that provide more functionality. Below the alternatives to Dropbox are described.

1. Pcloud

An inexpensive substitute to Dropbox is pCloud. Users can easily share the files from their smartphone by clicking the "Share" button; users can save their information in the official Pcloud for access in the future. The free plan for Pcloud offers a massive 10 Gb of storage, and they also provide new customers with an extra 5 Gb of storage space to new users who recommend friends to register for an account. Pcloud offers safe storage in the cloud for users' business data, videos, music, documents, and images. The file is encrypted using TLS/SSL after being transmitted to storage. Files are kept on three or more servers simply because security is a top priority.

Pros:

  1. Strong security options.
  2. There are numerous options for file-sharing.
  3. Accessibility across several devices.
  4. Unlimited file size.
  5. Password protection.

Cons:

  1. There are additional costs for Pcloud crypto.
  2. Customer-side encryption.
  3. Zero-knowledge privacy.
  4. It could be difficult to find files.

2. Google Drive

Users can store their data on the cloud with Google Drive and crucially sync these files and settings across numerous devices. It allows you to back up your files so they won't ever be lost. Google Drive offers 15 GB of storage.

Pros:

  1. Your data is secure and encrypted.
  2. Syncs desktop-to-desktop.
  3. Cross-platform capability.

Cons:

  1. No plans exist for limitless cloud storage.
  2. Google account required for best usage.

Price and plans:

  1. The monthly price for the Basic plan is $ 1.99, and it offers 100-gigabyte of storage.
  2. The monthly price for the Standard plan is $ 2.99, and it offers 200-gigabyte of storage.

3. Internxt

In terms of security and privacy, Dropbox's best substituted is Internxt. Internxt is a blockchain-based open-source cloud storage platform. It allows users to access a collection of apps suitable for all of their devices. Drive, Photos, and Send, are the three privacy-focused services that Internxt offers. End-to-end encryption is used to protect any file that is posted or shared using the service, and it also ensures that all first and third parties users cannot access the files.

Pros:

  1. Completely transparent and open-source.
  2. Unauthorized access to your data is not permitted.
  3. All information is end-to-end encrypted when it is submitted, stored, or shared.
  4. Free premium 10-gigabyte plan.

Cons:

  1. Integration issues with some third-party applications.

Price and plans:

  1. The free plan includes 10-gigabyte of cloud storage.
  2. The monthly price for a two-terabyte plan is $ 11.50.
  3. The monthly cost for a 200-gigabyte plan is $ 5.15.
  4. The monthly price for a 20-gigabyte plan is $ 1.15.

4. Box

The Box is a cloud storage platform for teams and businesses. It comes with a number of tools and features that can help users maximize productivity and improve their communication and collaboration with their teams, clients, partners, and vendors.

Pros:

  1. One location for work assignments, file editing, review, and sharing.
  2. There are more than 1500 business app connectors.
  3. Password-protected files and enterprise-grade protection.
  4. Can transfer large files.
  5. It enables the admin to manage the version and include outside people to participate in the content.

Cons:

  1. There are no permanent storage options.

5. Sync

Sync allows users to save, share, and access files from any device at anytime. End-to-end encryption is used to protect files. Sync-free software is available for Windows, macOS, iOS, Android, and the web. Paid packages for teams begin at $ 60 annually per user.

Pros:

  1. Centralized storage.
  2. All Sync plans come with built-in SOCs and end-to-end encryption.
  3. There won't be any third-party monitoring (The only legitimate owner of your data is you).
  4. In-house support.
  5. Users can transmit files to non-Sync users.

Cons:

  1. No plans exist for limitless cloud storage.
  2. Fewer storage options.

6. Tresorit

Tresorit is a Swiss online storage platform. Tresorit is a secure location to store critical files and folders on the cloud. The main clients of Tresorit are companies and collaborative teams.

Pros:

  1. Secure file sharing is made possible through encrypted links within and outside of your organization.
  2. Cloud storage with end-to-end encryption, zero-knowledge.
  3. Available in different languages.
  4. The user interface is easy to use.
  5. Free trial for 14 days
  6. Accessible on every mobile device and web browser.
  7. Two-factor authentication.

Cons:

  1. No plans exist for limitless cloud storage.
  2. Ransomware detection, file recovery, and password-protected sharing links are not included in the free plan.
  3. Fewer storage options.

7. MEGA

MEGA offers secure cloud storage that is user-controlled. It is accessible through web browsers and specific mobile apps. Here end-to-end encryption is used to protect files. Users can sync files and folders with this free cloud storage service. 50 GB of storage are provided without charge, and its premium plan offers 200 GB at $ 4.99 per month. MEGA is accessible on the Windows, Mac, and Linux operating systems. It is a great option for small businesses because it has enough storage space.

Pros:

  1. End-to-end AES-128 security, TLS GDPR compliant for all users worldwide.
  2. Two-factor authentication.
  3. Both free and premium plans are offered.
  4. Numerous storage options are available.
  5. Every year, transparency reports are released.

Cons:

  1. Complicated free plan.
  2. It's not open source.
  3. There are limited opportunities for cooperation.

8. Nextcloud

Nextcloud is a self-hosted productivity solution that enables users to collaborate on projects, send and receive emails, manage calendars, and offers video conversations feature without disclosing their personal information.

Pros:

  2. Free and open-source applications.
  3. Sharing files is made simple.
  4. The GDPR-compliant cloud solution.
  5. The feature of version tracking is optional.

Cons:

  1. Sometimes, the web interface is slow.
  2. Clunky sometimes.

9. Apple iCloud Drive

The Apple iCloud Drive is a simple option for online cloud storage for people who exclusively use Apple products. For Windows users, this is not the greatest option due to a few drawbacks. In this, encryption is used to protect user data. Its iCloud Private Relay feature hides IP addresses to keep users anonymous online.

Pros:

  1. Excellent mobile and desktop connection.
  2. Sleek, polished user interfaces.

Cons:

  1. Choices are limited for power users.
  2. Lack of advanced features.
  3. No Android support.

10. NordLocker

NordLocker is the best alternative for Dropbox. Secure cloud storage is provided by NordLocker. NordLocker was developed by Nord Security, the company that also developed NordVPN, NordPass, and NordLayer. NordLocker offers cutting-edge encryption and a strong zero-knowledge policy. The most effective encryption methods and advanced cyphers are used by NordLocker.

Pros:

  1. 3 GB of safe cloud storage are provided without any charge to NordLocker subscribers.
  2. It's possible to add files to local and cloud lockers.

Cons:

  1. No plans exist for limitless cloud storage.

Price and plans:

  1. The free plan includes a 3-gigabyte of cloud storage.
  2. The monthly price for a 500-gigabyte plan is $ 3.19.
  3. The monthly price for a two-terabyte plan is $ 7.99.

11. MediaFire

File hosting, cloud storage, and sync services are provided by MediaFire. MediaFire allows users to share documents, photographs, videos, and audio. It offers a simple method for managing digital items online. This tool supports many different types of files. This tool allows you to store all of your files easily and access them from anywhere. This tool allows users to send files via links.

Pros:

  1. Uploading huge files is permitted.
  2. Excellent transfer rates.
  3. Small interface.
  4. A trustworthy and safe platform.
  5. Non-technical staff members can also use it easily.
  6. The consumption-based model is affordable.
  7. Periodic virus scans.
  8. It gives you the option to specify access restrictions.
  9. For workers, it provides a professional, ad-free experience.

Cons:

  1. App frequently hangs.
  2. Files are not encrypted.
  3. Average customer service.
  4. Fewer integrations.

Price and plans:

  1. The price for the Pro plan is $ 3.75 per user per month.
  2. The price for a Business plan is $ 40 for 100 users per month.
  3. Ten gigabytes of storage are provided for free users.

12. Cloudup

An excellent Dropbox substitute for organizations and individuals is Cloudup. Although the tool is not good and effective as some other file storage tools, its simple sharing process makes it a strong candidate to be taken into consideration when you search for a Dropbox substitute. Two hundred gigabytes of storage are provided for free to users.

Pros:

  1. Quick sharing using links.
  2. Simple drag-and-drop files.
  3. A lot of free storage.
  4. Password security.
  5. Share and view files across any platform.

Cons:

  1. Sometimes speed becomes slow.
  2. Data hacking threat.
  3. Not providing enough assistance for the user.

13. Redbooth

Another well-known Dropbox substitute is Redbooth. This tool assists teams in task management and communication. Redbooth tool is used by companies like Coca-Cola and Starbucks and assists teams in managing multiple activities at once.

Pros:

  1. Low monthly cost.
  2. An interface is well-designed.
  3. Effortless and seamless project management.
  4. Containing a variety of thoughtful characteristics.
  5. Easy to use.
  6. Encourages teamwork.

Cons:

  1. Weak mobile app.
  2. Not providing enough assistance for the user.
  3. Limited features.

Price and plans:

  1. The price for the Pro plan is $ 9 per user per month.
  2. The price for the Business plan is $ 15 per user per month.

14. Quip

Quip is the productivity tool from Salesforce that makes it possible for companies to work together and facilitate modern collaboration across all devices in a secure and simple way.

Pros:

  1. Easy to set up.
  2. Backed by zapier.
  3. The free version is available.
  4. Robust communication features.
  5. Simple to use.
  6. Mobile-optimized.
  7. Information is easily available.

Cons:

  1. Lack of rich markup tools.
  2. Fewer integrations.
  3. The interface might be more advanced.
  4. No team calendar.

Price and plans:

  1. The price for a Quip starter is $ 10 per user per month.
  2. The price for the Quip Plus plan is $ 25 per user per month.
  3. The price for the Quip Advanced plan is $ 100 per user per month.

15. CloudMe

The best Dropbox substitute for companies and professionals is CloudMe. Users can sync documents and data between their laptops and mobile devices. By using this feature, you can easily send and receive files with friends, family, and coworkers. The top cloud/sync storage service in Europe is CloudMe, which provides a safe and rich user experience for all kinds of customers.

Pros:

  1. Advanced capabilities.
  2. Fast downloading.
  3. Numerous choices for sharing.
  4. File recovery.
  5. Supports both android and iOS.
  6. Sweden-based servers that prioritize privacy

Cons:

  1. The server does not encrypt the data.
  2. Slow uploading speed.

Price and plans:

  1. The monthly cost of the Team plan is 149 Euros for five users.
  2. The cost of the Business plan is 279 euros for 15 users.
  3. The cost of the Enterprise plan is 759 euros for 50 users.

16. Zoho Docs

Zoho Docs is the most underrated substitute for Dropbox. It is a flexible and effective online file management tool for groups and individuals. This tool allows you to carry all of your files with you everywhere you go, including documents, photographs, spreadsheets, presentations, music, and videos. You can create a great presentation with Zoho Docs as well. Built-in Zoho Office editors let you create, edit, and work with your coworkers.

Pros:

  1. File handling is simple.
  2. Outstanding online editing tools.
  3. Serves as a second drive for your files.
  4. Smart analytics and reporting.
  5. Chat within the app.
  6. File sharing and teamwork are simple.
  7. Includes Dropbox, google drive, and adobe sign integration.

Cons:

  1. Per-user charges can increase.
  2. Complex and poor integration.
  3. It's challenging to schedule calls.
  4. The mobile application is not present.
  5. Steep learning curve.

Price and plans:

  1. The price for the Standard plan is $ 4 per user per month.
  2. The price for the Premium plan is $ 6.4 per user per month.

17. SpiderOak

One of the best Dropbox substitutes is SpiderOak. This tool is the best choice for businesses because it is the first and only company that uses blockchain technology for security. This is a multi-user and multi-device backup tool with a centralized management console. This tool is quick, dependable, and safe for managing cloud data. SpiderOak places a strong emphasis on advanced security features, such as end-to-end encryption and zero-knowledge service.

Pros:

  1. It can be utilized on numerous devices.
  2. Sync capabilities.
  3. Fantastic speed.
  4. Provides support for an infinite number of computers per account.
  5. Excellent security and privacy.
  6. Professional customer service.
  7. End-to-end encryption.
  8. A fully functional and well-designed desktop program.

Cons:

  1. Lack of phone or chat support.
  2. There are no limitless plans.
  3. Costly.

Price and plans:

  1. The cost of a personal plan is $ 69 per year for 150 Gigabytes of space.
  2. The cost of a personal plan is $ 149 per year for two-terabyte of space.
  3. The cost of a large personal plan is $ 320 per year for five-terabyte of space.

18. Amazon Drive

Amazon Drive is a cloud storage tool offered by Amazon. Amazon Drive offers safe file backups, simple sharing and preview, cloud storage, and photo printing via the Amazon Prints service. Your photos, videos, and data all are accessible whenever you need them on various devices.

Pros:

  1. It's easy to set up.
  2. Full folders can be uploaded.
  3. Automatic and frequent backups are performed.
  4. You can share your files in various ways, such as through links, emails, Facebook, and Twitter.

19. IceDrive

Another well-known Dropbox substitute is IceDrive. IceDrive is an excellent option for collaborating, saving, and sharing data.

Pros:

  1. Cloud storage services that operate without interruption need a lot of bandwidth.
  2. The special characteristic of Icedrive is the drive-mounting software.

Cons:

  1. Not allowed to download the full folder.
  2. In the free plan, client-side encryption is not available.

Price and plans:

  1. The cost of a lite plan is $ 1.67 per year for 15 Gigabytes of space.
  2. The cost of a pro plan is $ 4.17 per year for one terabyte of space.
  3. The cost of the pro plus plan is $ 15 per year for five-terabyte of space.
